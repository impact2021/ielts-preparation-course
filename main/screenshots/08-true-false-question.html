Reading Passage

Photosynthesis is the process by which plants convert light energy into chemical energy that is later released to fuel the plant's activities. This process involves the absorption of light by chlorophyll in the plant's leaves.

The process occurs in two main stages: the light-dependent reactions and the light-independent reactions (Calvin cycle). During the light-dependent reactions, energy from sunlight is captured and used to split water molecules, releasing oxygen as a byproduct.

The Calvin cycle uses the energy captured during the light-dependent reactions to fix carbon dioxide into glucose. This glucose then serves as an energy source for the plant and forms the basis of most food chains on Earth.

Environmental factors such as light intensity, carbon dioxide concentration, and temperature all affect the rate of photosynthesis. Understanding these factors is crucial for optimizing crop production in agriculture.
