Reading Passage

The Silk Road was not a single road but rather an extensive network of trade routes that connected the East and West for over 1,500 years. Beginning as early as the 2nd century BCE, this network facilitated not only the exchange of goods but also ideas, technologies, and cultural practices.

Merchants traveled along these routes, facing numerous hazards including bandits, deserts, and mountain passes. Despite these challenges, the Silk Road became instrumental in spreading Buddhism from India to China, and Chinese silk production to the Mediterranean world.

The Silk Road trade gradually declined after the 15th century with the development of maritime routes by European explorers. However, its legacy remains profound, having shaped the development of civilizations throughout Asia, Europe, and the Middle East.
