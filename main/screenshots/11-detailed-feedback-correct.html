The microbiome refers to the collective genomes of microorganisms that inhabit a particular environment, most commonly referring to the human gut microbiome. Comprising trillions of bacteria, viruses, and fungi, the microbiome plays a crucial role in human health and disease.

Recent research has established that the gut microbiome influences numerous physiological processes including digestion, immune function, and even mental health through the gut-brain axis. Dysbiosis, an imbalance in microbial communities, has been linked to various diseases including inflammatory bowel disease, obesity, and depression.

Dietary choices significantly impact microbiome composition. Diets rich in fiber and fermented foods promote beneficial microbial communities, while high-sugar and processed food diets can reduce microbial diversity and promote pathogenic organisms.

Understanding the microbiome has opened new therapeutic possibilities, including probiotics, prebiotics, and fecal microbiota transplantation for treating various medical conditions.
