Urban farming has emerged as a viable solution to food security and environmental sustainability challenges in cities worldwide. By converting vacant urban spaces into productive gardens, communities can increase access to fresh produce while reducing the carbon footprint associated with long-distance food transportation.

Rooftop gardens, vertical farms, and community gardens have demonstrated significant potential for food production in urban environments. These initiatives not only supply nutritious food but also improve air quality, reduce urban heat island effects, and provide mental health benefits to community members.

Despite their advantages, urban farms face challenges including limited land availability, soil contamination concerns, and regulatory obstacles. Successful urban farming requires collaboration between city governments, community organizations, and residents to overcome these barriers.
