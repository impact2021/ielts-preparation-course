Reading Passage

Climate change is one of the most pressing issues facing our world today. Rising global temperatures have led to increased melting of polar ice caps, resulting in rising sea levels that threaten coastal communities worldwide.

The primary cause of climate change is the emission of greenhouse gases, particularly carbon dioxide, from human activities such as burning fossil fuels for energy. These gases trap heat in the atmosphere, creating a greenhouse effect that warms the planet.

Scientists have established that global temperatures have risen approximately 1.1°C since pre-industrial times. This warming has profound implications for ecosystems, agriculture, and human societies across the globe.

To combat climate change, countries are adopting renewable energy sources such as solar and wind power. Additionally, reforestation efforts and carbon capture technologies are being implemented as part of comprehensive climate action plans.
