The Internet has revolutionized communication in the 21st century. Email, instant messaging, and video conferencing have transformed how people connect across distances, enabling real-time conversations regardless of geographical location.

Social media platforms have further amplified this transformation, allowing individuals to share information instantly with millions of people worldwide. These technologies have democratized communication, giving voice to ordinary people alongside traditional media outlets.

However, this rapid expansion of digital communication has raised concerns about privacy, misinformation, and digital addiction. Regulatory bodies worldwide are developing frameworks to address these challenges while preserving the benefits of digital connectivity.
