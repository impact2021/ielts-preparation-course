Renewable energy sources have become increasingly important in addressing climate change and reducing dependence on fossil fuels. Solar power, wind energy, and hydroelectric power are among the most widely used renewable technologies globally.

Solar photovoltaic systems convert sunlight directly into electricity. Over the past decade, the cost of solar panels has decreased significantly, making solar energy more accessible to both residential and commercial users.

Wind turbines harness kinetic energy from wind to generate electricity. Wind farms, both onshore and offshore, have expanded rapidly as governments invest in renewable energy infrastructure.

Hydroelectric power remains one of the largest sources of renewable electricity worldwide. Large dams and river-based systems generate consistent power supply with minimal environmental impact compared to fossil fuel alternatives.
