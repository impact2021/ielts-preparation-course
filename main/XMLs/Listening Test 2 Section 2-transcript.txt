<p>Good afternoon ladies and gentlemen and thanks very much for having me here to speak to you today on the topic of health and fitness. The focus of my talk today will be swimming and how it can not only keep you fit but also be a social occasion - even a bit of fun!</p>

<p>Now, as you probably all know, as we get older it is harder to stay in shape and keep fit. Options like <strong style="background-color: yellow;">[Q1: jogging]</strong> don't always suit older people because of the strain they can cause on your knees and other joints. In fact, there are many so-called 'healthy' fitness options that can actually do more damage than good because of this strain. With swimming, however, the water can help support your <strong style="background-color: yellow;">[Q2: weight]</strong>, but at the same time provides some resistance, which makes it an excellent form of exercise that does not strain joints and muscles, and has been found to be particularly useful for people suffering from arthritis.</p>

<p>A gentle swim or even walk in water has also been proven to reduce <strong style="background-color: yellow;">[Q3: blood pressure]</strong>, and studies have shown that 20 minutes in the pool three times a week can reduce blood pressure within a matter of just one month. Now some of you may be thinking that a swim in warmer weather is fine, but not so great in colder weather. Well in fact this is another of the advantages of swimming â€“ although it may be a little cold when you first get in, exercising in the water reduces the risk of <strong style="background-color: yellow;">[Q4: overheating]</strong> common with many other forms of exercises. Overheating can actually cause problems as there is the risk of getting muscle pain.</p>

<p>Before we take a short break, I'll just share a statistic with you. Just a few years ago, a study by McAllan Research centre found that of 200 people aged 60 plus who had done some regular exercise in the previous 12 months, over 70% had stopped due to injury. Now most of these injuries were caused by <strong style="background-color: yellow;">[Q5: falls]</strong> which occurred during exercising, even something as gentle as lawn bowling or walking. Now when you consider that falling during swimming is not even possible, I think that's a great reason to consider it!</p>

<p>Now, when I refer to swimming, I would just like to point out that you don't even need to be able to swim to take advantage of getting some exercise in the local swimming pool. There are lots of options available, so I'll start by talking about water walking. By wearing a specially designed <strong style="background-color: yellow;">[Q6: belt]</strong> with a section at the back that will keep you afloat, you can exercise at your own pace. All you need to do is move your legs as though you are walking. Because the water is carrying most of the weight, there is a lot less pressure and stress on your knees and legs, but at the same time it is a very good way of getting some exercise. Also, because water walking is not a fast activity, you can do it with a friend or small group, and can even have <strong style="background-color: yellow;">[Q7: a conversation]</strong> as you walk around the pool.</p>

<p>For those of you who are quite confident with swimming, you can of course use the lanes to swim laps. However, I would make a strong recommendation here that you don't <strong style="background-color: yellow;">[Q8: overdo]</strong> it on your first couple of visits.  We have found that people who build up their swimming lengths gradually, even if they only do five or 10 laps, end up going more often than people who rush and try and do 20 laps in the first visit and find it too hard.</p>

<p>So, moderation is the key here. Now, as most of us are already retired and work hours are no longer a problem, you will be able to go during the day where it is a lot less busy.</p>

<p>In addition to water walking and lane swimming, you can also join a fitness class. These are often referred to as aqua aerobics, and involve <strong style="background-color: yellow;">[Q9: gentle movements]</strong> which will be shown to you by your instructor. Most aqua aerobics classes will also involve the use of some weights, although I should say that these are generally very light!</p>

<p>Again, this is a great option for those of you who are not that confident in the water, because most aqua aerobics classes are held in <strong style="background-color: yellow;">[Q10: shallow]</strong> pools where the water is no more than 1.2 m deep. This means that you will be able to stand and don't have to worry about keeping afloat. Because aqua aerobics is done in groups, it can be a very sociable way to keep fit and do some exercise. The classes are often done with background music as well, so it makes it a little bit more interesting for people in the class.</p>
